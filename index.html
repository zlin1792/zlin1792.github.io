Language & Literacy Education

I am a scholar in language and literacy education whose work examines bilingualism and multilingualism, early literacy development, multimodal literacies, heritage language maintenance, and posthuman/post-qualitative methodologies.

About

My research explores how children and families mobilize languages, materials, bodies, spaces, emotions, and everyday practices in language and literacy acquisition. I am especially interested in Chinese-Canadian children’s heritage language and biliteracy development at home and across transnational, multilingual, and culturally diverse contexts.

My doctoral research, Rethinking Resources: A More-Than-Human Reconceptualization of Chinese-Canadian Children’s Heritage Language Practices at Home, investigates children’s heritage language resources from a posthuman perspective.
