About

My research explores how children and families mobilize languages, materials, bodies, spaces, emotions, and everyday practices in literacy learning. I am especially interested in Chinese-Canadian children’s heritage language and biliteracy development at home and across transnational, multilingual, and culturally diverse contexts.

My doctoral research, Rethinking Resources: A More-Than-Human Reconceptualization of Chinese-Canadian Children’s Heritage Language Practices at Home, investigates children’s heritage language resources from a posthuman perspective.

Research Interests
